## News Roundup

1. **Sunshine Milling cuts mealie meal prices by K40** — The milling company reduced the price of a 25kg breakfast mealie meal from K260 to K220, citing lower maize prices, falling diesel costs, a stronger kwacha, and supportive government policy. This is a direct, tangible benefit of kwacha appreciation for Zambian households. *(News Diggers, Jun 9)*

2. **Maize no longer profitable for smallholder farmers** — Agriculture expert Oliver Bulaya warns that despite Zambia projecting nearly 5 million tonnes of maize in 2026, soaring input costs (seed and fertilizer) are eroding profitability for small-scale growers. He called for government intervention to reduce input costs. *(Zambian Business Times, Jun 9)*

3. **BOZ USD/ZMW mid-rate 17.7050** — The Bank of Zambia quoted the USD at 17.7050 mid-rate on June 9. The kwacha has paused its rally near 2-year highs. YTD appreciation stands at ~21%. *(BOZ, Jun 9)*

4. **Economist challenges BoZ inflation projection** — An economist has cast doubt on the Bank of Zambia's 6-8% inflation target for Q1 2026, arguing structural economic weaknesses make the target unrealistic. *(Zambian Business Times, Jun 9)*
